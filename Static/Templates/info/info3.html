Hostile environment

Also known as "the red planet", Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun. It is a dusty, cold and desert world with a very thin atmosphere. This dynamic planet has seasons, polar ice caps, canyons, extinct volcanoes, and evidence that it was even more active in the past.
Days on Mars are longer than those here on Earth, taking a little more than 24 hours, while a full year can take up to 687 Earth days. Temperatures on the Martian surface rise to 20 ° C and drop to -153 ° C. From time to time, the wind on Mars is strong enough to create dust storms that cover much of the planet’s surface for long periods of time. Two of the problems rovers face when studying Mars are that circuits can freeze due to low temperatures and if a rover gets caught in a dust storm it can shut down due to inactivity because it cannot charge its solar energy panels.
Unfortunately, so far there are no effective methods to avoid these situations. Imagine that you are exploring Mars with a rover, what tools do you think are essential for your survival in such adverse conditions?
